Why does a URL shortener need entropy?

Honestly, it didn't. But where's the fun in that?

I was bored one day and thought, why not build a URL shortener? Good Flask practice, simple project. Wrote the whole backend, and just as I was about to jump on the frontend, I noticed the short codes were just sequential numbers. `1`, `2`, `3`. Completely predictable. Anyone could just guess other people's links.

That got me thinking about randomness. Which led me down a rabbit hole to Cloudflare's lava lamp wall they film actual physical lava lamps to generate cryptographic entropy that secures a chunk of the internet. Real chaos, turned into keys.

I can't film lava lamps. So I built one in your browser instead. Every time you shorten a link, Ember samples the live pixel state of the animation — blobs mid-merge, mid-drift — and mixes that into the short code seed. It's pseudo-random, not true entropy. I know that. But it's a better story than `id + 1`.

The UI? I'm genuinely bad at frontend and find it kind of boring to write — so I had some help there. The backend though, that's all mine. Wrote every line of Flask myself, learning as I went.

That's it. That's the whole story.
